# Dividend Policy – Chính Sách Cổ Tức

> **Mục tiêu**: Hiểu các lý thuyết chính sách cổ tức (M&M Irrelevance, Bird-in-Hand, Tax Preference, Signaling), phương pháp chi trả, và ảnh hưởng đến giá trị doanh nghiệp.

---

## I. ĐỊNH NGHĨA VÀ LÝ THUYẾT NỀN TẢNG

### 1. Định nghĩa

**Dividend Policy** là quyết định về **tỷ lệ lợi nhuận chia cho cổ đông** (dưới dạng cổ tức) vs **giữ lại tái đầu tư** (retained earnings) trong doanh nghiệp.

> **Câu hỏi cốt lõi**: Nên trả bao nhiêu % lợi nhuận cho cổ đông? Trả theo hình thức nào? Tần suất ra sao? Cổ tức có ảnh hưởng giá cổ phiếu không?

$$\text{Net Income} = \text{Dividends Paid} + \text{Retained Earnings}$$

$$\text{Payout Ratio} = \frac{Dividends}{Net Income}$$

$$\text{Retention Ratio} = 1 - Payout Ratio = \frac{Retained Earnings}{Net Income}$$

### 2. Các hình thức chi trả cổ tức

| Hình thức | Mô tả | Ví dụ |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Cash Dividend** | Tiền mặt/chuyển khoản | 2,000 VND/cổ phiếu |
| **Stock Dividend** | Cổ phiếu thưởng | 10% stock dividend = 10 CP → nhận thêm 1 CP |
| **Share Buyback** | Mua lại cổ phiếu | Apple buyback $90B/year |
| **Special Dividend** | Cổ tức đặc biệt (1 lần) | Costco special $15/share |
| **Property Dividend** | Tài sản (hiếm) | Spin-off subsidiary shares |

## II. PHÂN TÍCH ĐỊNH LƯỢNG

### A. M&M Dividend Irrelevance Theorem (1961)

#### Statement
Trong thị trường hoàn hảo (no taxes, no transaction costs, symmetric info):

> **Giá trị DN chỉ phụ thuộc vào earning power và rủi ro kinh doanh, KHÔNG phụ thuộc dividend policy.**

#### Logic: "Homemade Dividends"
- Nếu DN không trả cổ tức → Investor bán một phần CP để "tạo" thu nhập
- Nếu DN trả nhiều cổ tức hơn muốn → Reinvest excess dividends vào stock
- → Investor tự điều chỉnh → Dividend policy irrelevant

#### Assumptions (strict):
1. No taxes (income tax = capital gains tax)
2. No transaction costs
3. No asymmetric information
4. Rational investors
5. No agency costs

→ **Trong thực tế**: Tất cả giả định đều KHÔNG đúng 100% → Dividend DOES matter!

---

### B. Bird-in-Hand Theory (Gordon & Lintner)

$$K_e = \frac{D_1}{P_0} + g$$

- Investors perceive dividends as LESS RISKY than future capital gains
- "A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush"
- → Firms paying higher dividends → Lower Ke → Higher valuation

#### Controversy
M&M counter: Risk depends on business operations (cash flows), not how you SPLIT them between dividends and retained earnings. A dollar of dividend comes from same risky business as a dollar of capital gain.

---

### C. Tax Preference Theory

| | Cash Dividends | Capital Gains |
|-|---------------|---------------|
| Timing | Taxed when received | Taxed when REALIZED (sell) |
| Rate (US) | Up to 20% + 3.8% NIIT | 0% / 15% / 20% (long-term) |
| Deferral | No | Yes (hold → defer tax) |
| Step-up at death | No | Yes (US: inherit at market value) |
| **Vietnam** | 5% PIT on dividends | 0.1% on sale proceeds |

→ In most countries: Capital gains taxed LESS → Investors prefer buybacks > dividends

---

### D. Signaling Theory

#### Dividend Signals

| Event | Market Reaction | Why |
|-------|----------------|-----|
| Dividend INITIATION | Stock ↑ 1-3% | "Management confident" |
| Dividend INCREASE | Stock ↑ 1-2% | "Earnings sustainable/growing" |
| Dividend MAINTAINED | Neutral | Expected |
| Dividend CUT | Stock ↓ 5-10%+ !! | "Management DESPERATE!" |
| Dividend OMISSION | Stock ↓ 10-25%+ !!! | "Company in serious trouble" |

#### Lintner's Model (1956)
Managers follow "partial adjustment" to target payout:

$$\Delta D_t = c \times (Target\,D_t - D_{t-1}) + \varepsilon$$

- Speed of adjustment $c$ ≈ 0.3-0.5 (slowly adjust)
- Managers **smooth** dividends (không tăng/giảm đột ngột)
- → VERY reluctant to CUT (vì signal quá negative)

---

### E. Share Buyback vs Cash Dividend

| Criteria | Cash Dividend | Share Buyback |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Tax** | Taxed immediately | Tax deferred (capital gain when sell) |
| **Flexibility** | Expected regularly (sticky) | Optional, one-time OK |
| **Signal** | "Confident about earnings" | "Stock undervalued" hoặc excess cash |
| **EPS impact** | Neutral | EPS ↑ (fewer shares) |
| **Who benefits** | ALL shareholders equally | Remaining shareholders |
| **Management preference** | Committed | Flexible (cancel anytime) |
| **Trend (1990-2024)** | Declining | GROWING (now > dividends in US!) |

#### S&P 500 Buyback vs Dividends (2023)

| | Buybacks | Dividends | Total |
|-|----------|-----------|-------|
| Amount | $795B | $588B | $1,383B |
| % of Net Income | 52% | 38% | 90% |

---

### F. Sustainable Growth Rate

$$g = ROE \times b$$

Trong đó: $b$ = Retention ratio = 1 - Payout ratio

| Payout Ratio | b (retention) | ROE | Sustainable g |
|-------------|--------------|-----|--------------|
| 30% | 70% | 20% | **14%** |
| 50% | 50% | 20% | **10%** |
| 80% | 20% | 20% | **4%** |
| 100% | 0% | 20% | **0%** (no growth!) |

→ **Trade-off**: High dividend = low retention = low sustainable growth rate!

## III. ỨNG DỤNG VÀ HOÀN CẢNH ÁP DỤNG

### Dividend Policy Decision Framework

```
SHOULD DN PAY HIGH DIVIDENDS?

YES (High Payout):                    NO (Low Payout / Buyback):
• Mature, stable business             • High-growth stage
• Limited growth opportunities         • Many profitable projects (NPV > 0)  
• Excess free cash flow               • Cash needed for investment
• Shareholders = retirees/funds       • Shareholders prefer capital gains
• Industry norm (utilities, REIT)      • Industry norm (tech)
• Signal confidence                    • Tax disadvantage (dividend tax high)
• Reduce agency costs (FCF)           • Maintain financial flexibility

        ┌─────────────────────┐
        │ LIFE CYCLE THEORY   │
        ├─────────────────────┤
        │ Startup: 0% payout  │ ← All retained, burning cash
        │ Growth:  0-20%      │ ← Need capital for investment
        │ Mature:  30-60%     │ ← Stable, moderate growth
        │ Decline: 60-100%+   │ ← No projects, return to shareholders
        └─────────────────────┘
```

### Dividend Yield vs Growth

| Company Type | Dividend Yield | Payout | Growth |
|-------------|---------------|--------|--------|
| High-tech growth | 0-1% | 0-20% | 20-40%/yr |
| Large-cap growth | 1-2% | 20-35% | 10-15%/yr |
| Blue-chip stable | 2-4% | 40-60% | 5-10%/yr |
| Utilities/REIT | 4-8% | 70-95% | 2-5%/yr |
| Distressed/Turnaround | 0% (cut) | 0% | Negative/turnaround |

## IV. ƯU ĐIỂM VÀ NHƯỢC ĐIỂM

### ✅ Ưu điểm trả cổ tức:
1. **Signal confidence** – "Chúng tôi đủ khỏe để chia tiền"
2. **Attract income investors** – Pension funds, retirees NEED income
3. **Discipline** – Giảm free cash flow → Managers cannot waste
4. **Bird-in-hand** – Certain income vs uncertain capital gains
5. **Stock price support** – Yield attracts buyers, provides floor
6. **Tax-free for some** – VN: Corporate investors 0% tax on dividends received

### ❌ Nhược điểm:
1. **Tax inefficiency** – Personal tax on dividends (5% VN, up to 23.8% US)
2. **Reduces flexibility** – Once set, very hard to cut (market punishes!)
3. **Opportunity cost** – Money paid out ≠ money invested in growth
4. **Growth limitation** – High payout → low retention → slow growth
5. **Sticky** – Must maintain even in bad years (signal pressure)
6. **Double taxation** – Corporate tax + Personal tax (in some jurisdictions)

## V. CASE STUDY THÀNH CÔNG: Berkshire Hathaway – ZERO Dividend, Maximum Value

### Bối cảnh
- Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway: **NEVER paid a cash dividend** (since 1967!)
- Market cap: ~$900B+ (2024)
- Compounded at 19.8%/yr for 58 years

### Buffett's Reasoning Against Dividends

| Argument | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Tax penalty** | $1 dividend → 23.8% tax → shareholder keeps $0.76. $1 retained → full $1 compounded |
| **Reinvestment opportunity** | Buffett ROE >> market return → Better to reinvest internally |
| **Rational allocation** | "I can allocate $1 better than market can" → True historically |
| **Homemade dividends** | "Sell shares if you need income" → Tax-efficient |
| **Flexibility** | No dividend commitment → Deploy capital opportunistically |

### The Math: Why No Dividend = More Value

Starting point: $1,000 invested in BRK (1965)

| Scenario | Year 58 Value (2023) | Annual Return |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|
| BRK (no dividend, reinvest all) | **$37,000,000+** | 19.8%/yr |
| If BRK paid 30% payout (taxed 23.8%) | ~$12,000,000 | ~17.2%/yr |
| S&P 500 (with reinvested dividends) | ~$330,000 | 10.2%/yr |

→ No dividend + superior capital allocation = **$25M+ additional value per $1,000!**

### But... 2024 Update
- Berkshire started BUYBACKS ($9B/year) – Tax-efficient way to return capital
- Reason: $150B+ cash pile, fewer attractive investments
- Still no dividend (sticky, tax-inefficient)

### Bài học
1. For exceptional capital allocators, retention >> dividend distribution
2. Tax compounding effect is MASSIVE over decades
3. Buybacks > dividends when stock is undervalued
4. ONLY works if company genuinely reinvests well (most companies DON'T)

## VI. CASE STUDY THẤT BẠI: GE – Dividend Cut = Destruction of Trust

### Bối cảnh
- General Electric: Premier "dividend aristocrat" (tăng cổ tức liên tục 30+ năm)
- Dividend yield ~3-4%, beloved by retirees and pension funds
- 2009: First dividend CUT in 71 years (từ $1.24 → $0.40/share, -68%)
- 2018: Second massive cut (từ $0.48 → $0.04, -92%!)

### Timeline of Disaster

| Year | Annual Dividend | Stock Price | Yield | Signal |
|------|----------------|------------|-------|--------|
| 2007 | $1.24 | $42 | 3.0% | All clear |
| 2009 | $0.40 (-68%) | $15 | 2.7% | Crisis! First cut in 71 yrs |
| 2017 | $0.48 | $30 | 1.6% | "Restructuring, but stable" |
| 2018 | $0.04 (-92%!) | $8 | 0.5% | Catastrophic signal |
| 2021 | $0.08 | $12 | 0.7% | Broken trust |
| 2024 | Split into 3 companies | - | - | End of GE as we knew it |

### Why the Dividend Cut Was Devastating

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Signaling** | 71-year streak broken → "Things are FAR worse than disclosed" |
| **Clientele shock** | Retirees who depended on income → Forced to sell → Price cascades |
| **Credibility destroyed** | CEO maintained "dividend is safe" weeks before cut → Lost all trust |
| **Institutional mandate** | Some funds MUST sell when dividend cut (dividend-focused mandates) |
| **Self-fulfilling spiral** | Cut → investors flee → stock drops → credit downgrades → worse |

### Root Cause Analysis
- GE Capital hidden losses (insurance reserves $15B+ shortfall)
- Acquisitions destroyed value (Alstom, Baker Hughes)
- Earnings management for years → Dividend paid from UNSUSTAINABLE earnings
- "Dividend was the last emperor's clothing" → Cut revealed naked emperor

### Bài học thất bại

```
1. SUSTAINABILITY > LEVEL: Only pay what you can sustain long-term
   → Payout ratio should leave margin of safety

2. NEVER PROMISE WHAT YOU CAN'T KEEP: GE's "safe" assurance weeks 
   before cut = destroyed credibility permanently

3. DIVIDEND IS A COMMITMENT: Once you start, market EXPECTS continuation
   → Think carefully before initiating

4. KNOW YOUR SHAREHOLDER BASE: If investors are income-dependent,
   a cut causes forced selling cascade

5. FIX THE BUSINESS FIRST: Don't use dividends to mask deterioration
   → Cash flow → Investment → THEN dividend
```

## VII. LƯU Ý VỀ PHƯƠNG PHÁP

### Dividend Policy Selection Guide

| Factor | Impact on Policy |
|--------|-----------------|
| Tax environment | High dividend tax → Prefer buybacks |
| Shareholder composition | Retirees → High dividend. Institutions → Buyback/low |
| Growth opportunities | High growth → Retain. Low growth → Distribute |
| Cash flow stability | Stable → Can commit dividend. Volatile → Buyback (flexible) |
| Industry norms | Follow peers unless strong reason to deviate |
| Legal constraints | REITs must pay 90%+. Loan covenants may restrict |
| Company life cycle | Startup 0% → Growth 0-20% → Mature 30-60% → Decline 60-100% |

### Key Formulas

| Formula | Use |
|---------|-----|
| $Dividend\,Yield = D_{annual} / P_0$ | Yield for investors |
| $Payout\,Ratio = D / EPS$ | % earnings distributed |
| $g = ROE \times (1 - Payout)$ | Sustainable growth |
| $P_0 = D_1 / (K_e - g)$ | Gordon Growth Model |
| $Buyback\,Yield = Buybacks / Market Cap$ | Total shareholder yield |
| $Total\,Yield = Div\,Yield + Buyback\,Yield$ | True return to shareholders |
